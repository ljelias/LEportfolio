A bit about me...

When life forced me to finally face the reality that I needed a new career avenue with better employment prospects, web development was an easy choice. I had considered majoring in computer science as an undergrad, and totally enjoyed a programming class I took. Since I already knew the basics of HTML, I didn't hesitate to jump into a 12-week full-stack web development bootcamp at the University of Minnesota. Then I went on to the Engineering Immersion program at Thinkful to further develop my skills.

Considering that I have a wide range of interests, I can envision my web development career path potentially taking several different directions...

My years of experience in higher education have given me an insider perspective of the educational technology needs of post-secondary students and faculty. I have used a range of technology applications, both good and not-so-good, so I bring a lot to the table from an authentic UX perspective. My field is linguistics and language education, and I believe there is still plenty of room for development in the area of language instruction applications.

My speciality is phonetics-phonology-pronunciation, and so another area that fascinates me is speech recognition/production. I have a knack for analyzing pronunciation patterns, and my ear automatically tunes in to computer generated speech ...whether I want to or not! As I instinctively analyze the unnatural sounding features of techno-voices, I often think things such as: "I wonder if they realize that in 'X' context, 'Y' sound is pronounced as 'Z'...?" I am sure I have much to offer in the realm of computer-generated speech.
