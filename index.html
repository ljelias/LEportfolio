A bit about me...

When life forced me to finally admit that I needed a new career avenue with better employment prospects, web development was an easy choice. I had considered majoring in computer science as an undergrad, and totally enjoyed a programming class I took. Having already learned the basics of HTML, I didn't hesitate to jump into a 12-week full-stack web development bootcamp at the University of Minnesota. From there I went on to the engineering immersion program at Thinkful to further develop my skills.

Considering that I have a wide range of interests, I can envision my web development career path potentially taking several different directions...

My years of experience in higher education has given me an insider perspective of the educational technology needs of post-secondary students and faculty. I have dealt with a range of technology applications, both good and not-so-good, so I bring a lot to the table from an authentic UX perspective. My field is linguistics and language education, and I believe there is still plenty of room for development in the area of language instruction applications.

My speciality is in phonetics/phonology/pronunciation, so an area that fascinates me is speech recognition and production. I have a knack for analyzing pronunciation patterns, and my ear automatically tunes in to computer generated speech, whether I want to or not! As I instinctively analyze the unnatural sounding aspects of the techno-voices, I often think things such as: "Don't they realize that in "X" context, "Y" sound is pronounced as "Z"...?" I am sure I have much to bring to the table in the realm of computer-generated speech.
