A bit about me...

I first completed a 12-week full-stack web development bootcamp and the University of Minnesota, and then went on to the engineering immersion program at Thinkful to further develop my skills. I have a wide range of interests, so my I can envision my web development career path potentially taking several different directions.

My years of experience in higher education has given me an insider perspective of the educational technology needs of post-secondary students and faculty. I have dealth with a range of technology applications, both good and not-so-good, so I bring a lot to the table from an authentic UX perspective. My field is linguistics and language education, and I believe there is still plenty of room for development in the area of language instruction applications.

My speciality is in phonetics/phonology/pronunciation, so an area that fascinates me is speech recognition and production. I have a knack for analyzing speech and pronunciation patterns, and my ear automatically tunes in to computer generated speech --whether I want to or not! I instinctively start to analyze the patterns behind the unnatural sounding parts, and as I listen to the techno-voices, I often think things such as: "Don't they realize that in "X" context, "Y" sound is pronounced as "Z"...?" I am sure I have much to bring to the table in the realm of computer-generated speech.
