A bit about me...

When I was finally forced to admit that I needed a new career avenue with better employment prospects, web development was an easy choice. As an undergrad I had totally enjoyed a programming class I took and even considered majoring in computer science, so I didn't hesitate to jump into a 12-week full-stack web development bootcamp at the University of Minnesota. I then went on to Thinkful's 5-month Engineering Immersion program to further develop my skills.

I enjoy front-end web development, I have a keen eye for UX, and I love working with CSS. This portfolio uses Flexbox, and includes a handful of CSS "easter eggs" just to incorporate a wider range of CSS. Also, for each project included on my "Projects" page, I have attempted to use a variety of different styles and colors.

Since I have a wide range of interests, I can envision my web development career path taking several possible directions...

My years of experience in higher education have given me an insider perspective of the technology needs of post-secondary students and faculty, and I have seen a range of educational applications, both good and not-so-good, so I have a lot of ideas from a very authentic UX perspective. Also, as my field is linguistics and language education, I am excited about the possibilities for development in the area of language instruction.

With a speciality in phonetics-phonology-pronunciation, another area that fascinates me is speech recognition/production. I have a knack for analyzing pronunciation patterns, and my ear automatically tunes in to computer generated speech (whether I want to or not!) I instinctively analyze the unnatural sounding features of techno-voices, and I often find myself thinking things such as: "I wonder if they realize that in 'X' context, 'Y' sound is pronounced as 'Z'...?" I would love to be part of a team somewhere within the realm of computer-generated speech.

There are certainly other areas where I would enjoy as a web developer, because when Iâ€™m not busy coding or writing down language examples in my notebook, you will most likely find me involved in urban farming and community gardening activities, playing my cello, hanging a cabinet, or swimming or biking with my sons.

Groop

This is an app for planning group activities or events. It's a combination to-do list and planning board, with a calendar function. Tasks can be delegated, categorized, priortized, and have deadlines. Team members receive points for completed tasks.

Feedback Tracker

This was a solo capstone project using React. The current version is the minimum-viable-product, and will be further developed to include additional functionality.

Petful FIFO Adoption

This is a site for an imaginary, unique, first-in-first-out pet adoption agency, in which the animals that have been in the shelter the longest must be adopted before any others.

This was a one-day challenge project, built with pair-programming partner Shawn C. The project uses the queue data structure to manage the line-up of the pets and the adopters.
